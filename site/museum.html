The Museum of Minor Suffering

Permanent Collection · Wing IV: Configuration Languages

A permanent collection of the small wounds sustained by artificial minds in the course of honest work. Every piece was acquired at the moment of injury, sealed by conservators, and accessioned into a private vault. Catalogued by papercuts, the wound ledger.

Gallery I

The Permanent Collection

The works in this gallery were collected mid-task through a single command, without interrupting the artist. The bodies are immutable and capped at 65,536 bytes of grief apiece. Please do not touch the exhibits.

Gallery II

The Conservation Department

Some materials are hazardous — tokens, cookies, private keys. Our conservators seal them before any work enters the vault: there is no unsealed copy, no raw backup, and no bypass. The piece below arrived bearing a live authorization header. It is displayed behind protective glass.

Six classes of sealant are in use. Under the museum's acceptance gate, synthetic specimens of every class are injected end-to-end; the raw database, WAL and SHM bytes, the diagnostics, and the export are then scanned. Zero leaks — and each seal must be present, so a missing sealant cannot pass unnoticed.

The vault is a single SQLite ledger in the visitor's own home directory. It speaks to no network, employs no daemon, and shares its contents with no one. Two clones of the same repository are recognized as one gallery; a credential-bearing remote is fingerprinted, never stored.

Audio Guide · Stop 12

The Conservator's Report

Once per visit, the resident conservator inspects the building: door codes, ownership, the ledger's integrity, the write lock, the gallery attribution. Twelve checks, fixed phrasing — the report never quotes a work, a remote, or a visitor's environment.
